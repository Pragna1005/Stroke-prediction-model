Understanding Stroke Risk

A stroke occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, depriving brain cells of oxygen and nutrients. This can lead to cell death within minutes, making stroke a medical emergency requiring immediate attention.

Stroke is a leading cause of death and disability worldwide. Early identification of risk factors and proactive prevention can significantly reduce its incidence. Our tool helps individuals understand their risk and take informed steps toward prevention.

This application uses advanced machine learning models to assess stroke risk based on health and demographic data. By analyzing key factors, it provides personalized risk predictions tailored to different clinical contexts, such as emergency screening or general health assessments.

Key Risk Factors for Stroke

Age

The risk of stroke increases with age, particularly after 55, due to natural changes in blood vessels and heart function.

Hypertension

High blood pressure damages blood vessels, making them more prone to rupture or blockage, a major risk factor for stroke.

Glucose Levels

High blood sugar, as seen in diabetes, damages blood vessels and increases stroke risk. Proper management is crucial.

Heart Disease

Conditions like atrial fibrillation can cause clots that lead to stroke. Heart health management is key to prevention.

About Our Prediction System

Machine Learning Models

Our application uses a suite of machine learning models, including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Neural Networks, and more, optimized for different clinical contexts like emergency or general screening.

These models were selected for their accuracy, interpretability, and ability to handle diverse health data, ensuring reliable predictions tailored to your needs.
